BPC-157

Emerging Use of BPC-157 in Orthopaedic Sports Medicine: A Systematic Review
Source: PMC (PubMed Central) | Published: 2025
View Article →
Key Clinical Takeaways:
  • Systematic review identified 544 articles from 1993-2024, with only 1 clinical study and 35 preclinical studies included after screening
  • Shows promise for musculoskeletal recovery in animal models but lacks clinical safety data in humans
  • Demonstrates potential in healing muscle, tendon, ligament, and bone injuries through VEGF stimulation and angiogenesis
  • FDA classified as Category 2 bulk drug substance in 2023 - cannot be legally compounded due to insufficient safety data
  • WADA prohibited BPC-157 in 2022 under S0 Unapproved Substances category for all athletes
BPC-157: The peptide with big claims and scant evidence
Source: STAT News | Published: February 2026
View Article →
Key Clinical Takeaways:
  • Phase I clinical trial (NCT02637284) initiated in 2015 with 42 healthy volunteers was cancelled in 2016 without published results
  • Critical gap exists in human pharmacokinetic data and long-term safety information
  • Lack of patent protection makes commercial investment in extensive clinical trials less attractive
  • Theoretical cancer risk due to angiogenesis stimulation remains unknown without long-term human data
Every BPC-157 Human Trial Up to 2026: What the Research Actually Shows
Source: Peptide Database | Published: 2026
View Article →
Key Clinical Takeaways:
  • Only 3 small pilot studies exist: knee pain (16 patients, 87.5% relief), interstitial cystitis (12 women, 83% complete resolution), and IV safety study (2 adults)
  • All human studies lack placebo controls, have small sample sizes, and short follow-up periods
  • No registered clinical trials actively recruiting on ClinicalTrials.gov as of 2026
  • Proper Phase I, II, and III trials required before BPC-157 can become evidence-based therapy

LL-37

Antimicrobial peptide LL37 is potent against non-growing Escherichia coli cells
Source: mSphere (ASM Journals) | Published: January 2025
View Article →
Key Clinical Takeaways:
  • LL-37 potent against non-growing E. coli cells despite slower action rate - lower MBC for dormant cells suggests strong efficacy over time
  • Critical insight for targeting persistent bacterial infections where dormant cells contribute to antibiotic treatment failure
  • Attacks bacteria through physics-based mechanism: electrostatic and hydrophobic forces forming trans-membrane pores
  • Amphiphilic structure allows binding to bacterial membranes and penetration of lipid area to compromise cell integrity
The Potential of Human Recombinant Cathelicidin LL-37 in Treatment of Infections
Source: International Journal of Peptide Research and Therapeutics | Published: November 2025
View Article →
Key Clinical Takeaways:
  • Confirmed broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity against clinical bacterial strains from various patient infections
  • Natural resistance to LL-37 uncommon among bacterial pathogens across multiple studies
  • Can dissolve bacterial biofilms at significantly lower concentrations than required to kill constituent bacteria
  • 2024: LL-37-derived peptide completed Phase I/II clinical trials for antitumor effects in melanoma patients
Antimicrobial Peptide Biological Activity, Delivery Systems and Clinical Translation
Source: Journal of Translational Medicine (PMC) | Published: 2025
View Article →
Key Clinical Takeaways:
  • 2025: LL-37 undergoing clinical evaluation for chronic wounds, psoriasis, acne, and respiratory infections including antibiotic-resistant bacteria
  • Development of advanced delivery systems (nanoparticles, hydrogels) crucial for precise controlled release at infection sites
  • Researchers anticipate LL-37 fragments and analogs will eventually be developed into new anticancer drug for clinical use
  • 2025 research focuses on systematic modifications to address high production costs, reduced physiological efficacy, and cytotoxicity

# Gut Microbiome Restoration Protocol

## Overview

The gut microbiome is a complex ecosystem of trillions of microorganisms that plays a critical role in immune function, nutrient absorption, mental health, and overall wellness. This protocol provides a structured approach to restoring and maintaining optimal gut microbiome balance through evidence-based nutritional strategies, targeted supplementation, and lifestyle modifications.

## Phase 1: Assessment & Elimination (Weeks 1–3)

### Initial Assessment
- Comprehensive stool analysis (GI-MAP or equivalent)
- Food sensitivity testing (IgG/IgA panels)
- SIBO breath test if indicated
- Organic acids testing for yeast/fungal markers
- Symptom tracking journal initiation

### Elimination Protocol
Remove the following for a minimum of 21 days:
- Refined sugars and artificial sweeteners
- Processed foods and seed oils (canola, soybean, corn, sunflower)
- Gluten-containing grains
- Conventional dairy products
- Alcohol and caffeine
- Known individual food sensitivities

### Antimicrobial Support (if pathogenic overgrowth identified)
- Berberine: 500 mg 2–3x daily with meals
- Oregano oil: 150 mg emulsified, 2x daily between meals
- Caprylic acid: 1,000 mg 2x daily
- Allicin (stabilized garlic): 450 mg 2x daily
- Duration: 4–6 weeks under practitioner guidance

## Phase 2: Repair & Restore (Weeks 4–8)

### Gut Lining Repair
- L-Glutamine: 5–10 g daily (powder form, divided doses)
- Zinc carnosine: 75 mg 2x daily
- Aloe vera inner leaf: 1–2 oz daily
- Deglycyrrhizinated licorice (DGL): 400 mg before meals
- Collagen peptides: 10–20 g daily
- Butyrate: 300 mg 2x daily

### Anti-Inflammatory Support
- Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA/DHA): 2–3 g daily
- Curcumin (liposomal or with piperine): 500–1,000 mg daily
- Quercetin: 500 mg 2x daily
- N-Acetyl Cysteine (NAC): 600 mg 2x daily

### Digestive Enzyme Support
- Full-spectrum digestive enzymes with meals
- Betaine HCl with pepsin (if hypochlorhydria confirmed)
- Ox bile if fat malabsorption is present

## Phase 3: Reinoculation (Weeks 6–12)

### Probiotic Protocol (Phased Introduction)

**Week 6–7: Foundation Strains**
- Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG: 10 billion CFU daily
- Saccharomyces boulardii: 5 billion CFU daily

**Week 8–9: Diversity Building**
- Add Bifidobacterium longum: 5 billion CFU
- Add Lactobacillus plantarum: 5 billion CFU
- Soil-based organisms (Bacillus subtilis, B. coagulans): as directed

**Week 10–12: Maintenance Blend**
- Multi-strain probiotic: 50–100 billion CFU daily
- Rotate brands quarterly

### Prebiotic Foods (Gradual Introduction)
- Partially hydrolyzed guar gum (PHGG): start 3 g, increase to 6 g daily
- Inulin/FOS: start 2 g, increase to 5 g daily
- Resistant starch sources: cooled potatoes, green bananas, cooked-and-cooled rice
- Fermented foods: sauerkraut, kimchi, kefir, kombucha (introduce slowly)

## Phase 4: Maintenance & Optimization (Ongoing)

### Daily Dietary Framework
- 30+ different plant foods per week for microbial diversity
- Bone broth: 1–2 cups daily
- Fermented foods: 1–2 servings daily
- Prebiotic-rich foods at every meal
- Adequate fiber: 30–40 g daily from whole food sources
- Polyphenol-rich foods: berries, green tea, dark chocolate, olive oil

### Lifestyle Factors
- Stress management: daily meditation, breathwork, or prayer
- Sleep optimization: 7–9 hours, consistent schedule
- Regular physical activity: minimum 150 minutes moderate exercise per week
- Time in nature: earthing/grounding practices (see Earthing & Grounding Protocol)
- Minimize antibiotic use; discuss alternatives with practitioner
- Filter water to remove chlorine and fluoride

### Monitoring
- Repeat GI-MAP at 3-month and 6-month intervals
- Track symptom improvement using standardized questionnaire
- Adjust protocol based on test results and clinical response

## Special Considerations

### SIBO-Specific Modifications
- Low FODMAP diet during antimicrobial phase
- Prokinetic support (ginger, 5-HTP, or prescription)
- Elemental diet option for refractory cases (2–3 weeks)
- Address root causes: ileocecal valve dysfunction, motility disorders

### Candida Overgrowth Modifications
- Strict sugar and refined carbohydrate elimination
- Antifungal rotation: nystatin, fluconazole, or natural alternatives
- Biofilm disruptors: NAC, enzymes, EDTA
- See companion Candida Diet protocol from Diane the Dietician

### Histamine Intolerance Considerations
- Avoid high-histamine fermented foods initially
- Use histamine-degrading probiotic strains (L. rhamnosus, B. infantis)
- DAO enzyme supplementation with meals
- Gradual fermented food introduction after gut healing

## Contraindications & Warnings
- This protocol is educational and should be implemented under practitioner supervision
- Individuals on immunosuppressive therapy should consult their physician before probiotic use
- Herxheimer reactions may occur during antimicrobial phases; reduce dosing if severe
- Pregnant or nursing mothers should modify supplementation accordingly

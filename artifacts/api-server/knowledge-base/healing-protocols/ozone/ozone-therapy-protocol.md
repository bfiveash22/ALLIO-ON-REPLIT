# Ozone Therapy Protocol

## Overview

Medical ozone therapy utilizes a mixture of ozone (O₃) and oxygen (O₂) for therapeutic purposes. Ozone therapy has been used in clinical medicine for over a century, with extensive research documenting its applications in immune modulation, antimicrobial activity, tissue oxygenation, and wound healing. This protocol covers clinical and home-use applications within the FFPMA framework.

## Mechanism of Action

### How Ozone Works in the Body
1. **Oxidative preconditioning**: Low-dose ozone triggers the body's antioxidant defense systems (Nrf2 pathway activation)
2. **Immune modulation**: Stimulates cytokine production, activates monocytes and lymphocytes
3. **Antimicrobial**: Directly inactivates bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites through oxidative disruption
4. **Improved oxygenation**: Increases 2,3-DPG in red blood cells, enhancing oxygen release to tissues
5. **Circulation**: Improves blood rheology and microcirculation
6. **Mitochondrial support**: Enhances mitochondrial respiration and ATP production

### The Hormetic Response
- Ozone therapy works on the principle of hormesis—a controlled, mild oxidative stress that triggers powerful adaptive antioxidant responses
- The body responds by upregulating glutathione, superoxide dismutase (SOD), catalase, and other protective enzymes
- Net result: enhanced antioxidant capacity and resilience

## Clinical Protocols

### Protocol 1: Major Autohemotherapy (MAH)

**Procedure**
1. Draw 100–200 mL of patient blood into a heparinized or citrated collection bag
2. Add ozone/oxygen mixture at concentration of 20–40 mcg/mL
3. Gently mix blood with ozone gas for 3–5 minutes
4. Re-infuse ozonated blood back into patient via IV
5. Total procedure time: 30–60 minutes

**Dosing**
| Session | Ozone Concentration | Blood Volume |
|---------|---------------------|--------------|
| 1–3 | 20 mcg/mL | 100 mL |
| 4–6 | 30 mcg/mL | 150 mL |
| 7+ | 40 mcg/mL | 200 mL |

**Frequency**
- Acute conditions: 2–3x per week for 4–6 weeks
- Chronic conditions: 1–2x per week for 10–20 sessions
- Maintenance: Monthly or quarterly

**Indications**
- Chronic infections (Lyme, viral, fungal)
- Autoimmune conditions
- Chronic fatigue syndrome
- Fibromyalgia
- Cardiovascular disease support
- Cancer adjunctive therapy
- Mold illness/CIRS
- General immune optimization

### Protocol 2: Ten-Pass Hyperbaric Ozone (OHT)

**Procedure**
- Advanced MAH: Blood is drawn and ozonated under positive pressure 10 times in a single session
- Higher ozone doses achieved than standard MAH
- Requires specialized equipment (Herrmann Hyper-Medozon)
- Session duration: 60–90 minutes

**Indications**
- Severe chronic infections
- Lyme disease (especially chronic/late-stage)
- Cancer adjunctive care
- Severe autoimmune conditions
- Not a first-line protocol; use after establishing tolerance with standard MAH

### Protocol 3: Rectal Insufflation (Home Protocol)

**Equipment Needed**
- Medical-grade ozone generator with oxygen tank
- Silicone catheter (size 16–18 French)
- Silicone bag or syringe
- Water-based lubricant
- Timer

**Procedure**
1. Generate ozone at desired concentration into silicone bag
2. Lubricate catheter tip, insert 2–4 inches into rectum
3. Slowly release ozone/oxygen mixture over 2–5 minutes
4. Retain as long as comfortable (minimum 10–20 minutes)
5. Perform in the morning before eating, or 2+ hours after a meal

**Dosing Protocol (Graduated)**
| Week | Concentration | Volume | Frequency |
|------|--------------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | 15 mcg/mL | 150 mL | 3x/week |
| 2 | 20 mcg/mL | 200 mL | 3x/week |
| 3 | 25 mcg/mL | 250 mL | 3–5x/week |
| 4+ | 30–35 mcg/mL | 300 mL | 3–5x/week |
| Maintenance | 25–30 mcg/mL | 200–300 mL | 2–3x/week |

**Benefits**
- Systemic ozone delivery via portal vein to liver
- Liver detoxification support
- Gut pathogen elimination
- Immune stimulation
- Safe and effective home protocol

### Protocol 4: Ear Insufflation

**Procedure**
1. Set ozone generator to low concentration (15–20 mcg/mL)
2. Use modified stethoscope or ear insufflation adaptor
3. Insert adaptor loosely into ear canal
4. Allow ozone to flow gently for 3–10 minutes per ear
5. Start with shorter durations and lower concentrations

**Indications**
- Ear infections
- Sinus congestion
- Brain fog and cognitive issues
- Tinnitus (some cases)
- Headaches/migraines
- Upper respiratory infections

### Protocol 5: Limb Bagging

**Procedure**
1. Place affected limb in ozone-resistant bag (silicone or Teflon)
2. Seal bag around limb (not too tight)
3. Humidify skin with water (ozone needs moisture to penetrate)
4. Fill bag with ozone at 30–60 mcg/mL
5. Duration: 20–30 minutes
6. Frequency: Daily or as needed for wound healing

**Indications**
- Diabetic ulcers and non-healing wounds
- Peripheral neuropathy
- Skin infections (fungal, bacterial)
- Post-surgical wound healing
- Burns
- Gangrene prevention

### Protocol 6: Ozonated Water

**Preparation**
- Bubble ozone through cold, filtered water for 10–15 minutes
- Use immediately (ozone dissipates within 20–30 minutes)
- Glass container only (no plastic)

**Applications**
- Drink 8–16 oz on empty stomach (gut health, systemic)
- Oral rinse for dental health and gum disease
- Topical application for skin conditions
- Eye wash (very dilute) for conjunctivitis

### Protocol 7: Ozonated Olive Oil

**Preparation**
- Medical-grade olive oil ozonated for 2–4 weeks
- Available commercially (certified medical grade)
- Store in refrigerator for extended shelf life

**Applications**
- Topical: Skin infections, wounds, burns, eczema, psoriasis
- Vaginal: Yeast infections, bacterial vaginosis (suppositories)
- Rectal: Hemorrhoids, fissures (suppositories)
- Oral: Gum disease (apply to gums)

## Safety & Contraindications

### Absolute Contraindications
- G6PD deficiency (can cause hemolytic anemia)
- Hyperthyroidism (uncontrolled)
- Acute hemolytic anemia
- Active hemorrhage
- Pregnancy (first trimester)
- Ozone allergy (extremely rare)

### Relative Contraindications
- Severe anemia
- Recent myocardial infarction
- Organ transplant patients on immunosuppressants
- Thrombocytopenia (low platelets)

### Safety Precautions
- Never inhale ozone directly (respiratory irritant)
- Use ozone-resistant materials only (silicone, glass, Teflon)
- Ensure proper ventilation in treatment areas
- Catalytic ozone destructor on generator exhaust
- Medical-grade oxygen source (not air-fed generators for medical use)
- Proper training before home use
- Start with lower concentrations and increase gradually

### Side Effects (Usually Mild)
- Herxheimer reaction (die-off symptoms) during initial treatments
- Mild fatigue after treatment
- Temporary increase in symptoms before improvement
- Rectal cramping with insufflation (reduce volume/flow rate)
- Ear sensitivity with ear insufflation (reduce concentration/time)

## Equipment Recommendations

### Medical-Grade Ozone Generators
- Must produce precise, measurable ozone concentrations
- Oxygen-fed (not ambient air)
- Digital readout of gamma/mcg
- Recommended brands: Longevity Resources, Promolife, SimplyO3
- Calibrate regularly per manufacturer guidelines

### Oxygen Supply
- Medical-grade oxygen tank with regulator
- Flow rate: 1/8–1/2 LPM depending on application
- Secure tank properly; store upright
- Replace when tank reaches 200 PSI

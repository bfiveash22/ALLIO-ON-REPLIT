# Toxicology & Detoxification Protocols

## Overview

Modern environmental exposures—heavy metals, pesticides, industrial chemicals, mold mycotoxins, and electromagnetic radiation—place unprecedented toxic burdens on human physiology. This protocol provides a systematic approach to identifying toxic exposures, supporting the body's innate detoxification pathways, and implementing targeted removal strategies.

## Understanding Toxic Burden

### Common Environmental Toxins
- **Heavy metals**: Mercury (dental amalgams, fish), lead (old paint, pipes), cadmium (tobacco, rice), arsenic (water, rice), aluminum (cookware, antiperspirants)
- **Mold mycotoxins**: Ochratoxin A, aflatoxins, trichothecenes, gliotoxin
- **Pesticides/Herbicides**: Glyphosate, organophosphates, pyrethroids
- **Industrial chemicals**: BPA, phthalates, PCBs, dioxins, PFAS
- **Volatile organic compounds (VOCs)**: Formaldehyde, benzene, toluene

### The Body's Detox Systems
1. **Phase I liver detoxification**: Cytochrome P450 enzymes (oxidation, reduction, hydrolysis)
2. **Phase II liver conjugation**: Glutathione, methylation, sulfation, glucuronidation, acetylation, amino acid conjugation
3. **Phase III transport**: Cellular exporters moving conjugated toxins out
4. **Elimination pathways**: Kidneys (urine), bowels (stool), skin (sweat), lungs (breath)

## Assessment & Testing

### Recommended Testing Panel
- Urine toxic metals (provoked and unprovoked): Doctor's Data or Quicksilver Scientific
- Mycotoxin panel: Great Plains/Mosaic GPL-MycoTOX
- Environmental pollutants: GPL-TOX (toxic organic chemical profile)
- Glyphosate test: Great Plains
- Blood heavy metals panel
- Organic acids test (detox markers)
- Genetic testing: MTHFR, GST, SOD2, COMT polymorphisms

### Clinical Indicators of Toxic Burden
- Chronic fatigue unresponsive to treatment
- Brain fog, memory difficulties
- Multiple chemical sensitivities
- Unexplained neuropathy
- Hormonal imbalances resistant to treatment
- Chronic pain syndromes
- Recurrent infections
- Skin conditions: eczema, psoriasis, acne

## Phase 1: Drainage Pathway Preparation (Weeks 1–3)

Before mobilizing stored toxins, ensure all elimination pathways are functioning:

### Bowel Support
- Goal: 2–3 well-formed bowel movements daily
- Magnesium citrate or oxide: 400–800 mg at bedtime
- Ground flaxseed: 2 tbsp daily
- Adequate hydration: minimum half body weight in ounces
- Fiber: 30–40 g from whole food sources

### Liver Support
- Milk thistle (silymarin): 300 mg 2x daily
- Dandelion root: 500 mg 2x daily or as tea
- Artichoke extract: 500 mg daily
- Beet root powder: 1 tsp daily
- Bitter greens in diet: arugula, endive, radicchio

### Kidney Support
- Adequate filtered water intake
- Cranberry extract: 500 mg daily
- Parsley tea: 1–2 cups daily
- Reduce sodium intake
- Monitor GFR if chelation planned

### Lymphatic Support
- Dry brushing: daily before shower, toward heart
- Rebounding: 10–15 minutes daily
- Deep breathing exercises
- Manual lymphatic drainage massage monthly
- Movement and exercise

## Phase 2: Cellular Detoxification

### Glutathione Optimization
Glutathione is the master antioxidant and primary detox molecule:
- Liposomal glutathione: 500–1,000 mg daily
- NAC (N-Acetyl Cysteine): 600–1,200 mg daily
- Glycine: 3–5 g daily
- Alpha lipoic acid: 300–600 mg daily (R-lipoic preferred)
- Selenium: 200 mcg daily
- Vitamin C: 2–4 g daily (divided doses)

### Methylation Support
For those with MTHFR or methylation SNPs:
- Methylfolate: 400–800 mcg daily (start low)
- Methylcobalamin (B12): 1,000–5,000 mcg daily
- Trimethylglycine (TMG): 500 mg daily
- Riboflavin (B2): 25–50 mg daily
- Pyridoxal-5-phosphate (P5P): 25–50 mg daily

### Nrf2 Activation
Nrf2 pathway upregulates detoxification genes:
- Sulforaphane (broccoli sprout extract): 30–60 mg daily
- Curcumin: 500–1,000 mg daily
- Resveratrol: 200–500 mg daily
- Green tea extract (EGCG): 400 mg daily
- Exercise and cold exposure

## Phase 3: Targeted Detoxification Protocols

### Heavy Metal Chelation

**Oral Chelation (Mild–Moderate Burden)**
- Modified citrus pectin: 5–15 g daily
- Chlorella (broken cell wall): 3–6 g daily
- Cilantro tincture: 10–15 drops 2x daily (pair with binder)
- IMD (Intestinal Metals Detox): as directed by Quicksilver protocol
- EDTA suppositories: as prescribed

**IV Chelation (Moderate–Severe Burden)**
- See IV Therapy Standards document for protocols
- CaEDTA for lead and general metals
- DMPS for mercury
- Monitor renal function regularly
- Mineral replacement protocol essential

### Mold/Mycotoxin Detoxification

**Binders (take 2 hours away from food/supplements)**
- Activated charcoal: 500 mg 2x daily
- Bentonite clay: 1 tsp in water daily
- Cholestyramine: 4 g 2–4x daily (prescription)
- Welchol: 625 mg, 3 tablets 2x daily (alternative)
- Modified citrus pectin: 5 g 2x daily
- Saccharomyces boulardii: 5 billion CFU daily

**Antifungal Support**
- Glutathione (liposomal or IV): 500–2,000 mg daily
- NAC: 600 mg 2x daily
- VIP nasal spray: as prescribed (for CIRS)
- Omega-3 fatty acids: 3–4 g daily
- Phosphatidylcholine: 1,200 mg daily

**Environmental Remediation**
- Professional mold inspection and remediation
- HEPA air purifiers in all living spaces
- Dehumidifiers to maintain <50% humidity
- Remove water-damaged materials
- ERMI or HERTSMI-2 testing for home

### Glyphosate Detoxification
- Humic/fulvic acid: binds glyphosate in GI tract
- Glycine supplementation: 3–5 g daily (competitive displacement)
- Probiotic support (especially Lactobacillus strains)
- Organic diet strictly
- Sauna therapy for systemic clearance

## Detoxification Therapies

### Infrared Sauna Protocol
- Start: 15 minutes at 130°F
- Build to: 30–45 minutes at 140–160°F
- Frequency: 3–5x weekly
- Hydrate before and after (electrolytes)
- Shower immediately after to remove toxins from skin
- Niacin (100–500 mg) 30 minutes before session (flush form) for enhanced mobilization
- Take binders after sauna

### Coffee Enema Protocol
- Organic, medium-roast, SA Wilson's Gold Roast or equivalent
- Brew: 3 tbsp coffee in 1 quart filtered water, simmer 15 minutes, cool to body temperature
- Position: Right side lying, retain 12–15 minutes
- Frequency: 1–3x weekly during active detox
- Benefits: Stimulates bile flow, increases glutathione S-transferase activity by 600–700%
- Contraindications: Active GI bleeding, bowel obstruction, recent surgery

### Detox Baths
- Epsom salt: 2–4 cups (magnesium sulfate)
- Baking soda: 1 cup (alkalizing)
- Bentonite clay: 1/2 cup (toxin binding)
- Essential oils: lavender, frankincense (optional)
- Duration: 20–30 minutes
- Temperature: Warm, not hot (for sensitive individuals)
- Frequency: 2–3x weekly

### Oil Pulling
- Organic coconut or sesame oil: 1 tbsp
- Swish gently for 15–20 minutes each morning
- Spit into trash (not sink)
- Rinse mouth, brush teeth
- Pulls fat-soluble toxins from oral mucosa

## Lifestyle Modifications

### Reducing Toxic Exposure
- Switch to organic produce (especially Dirty Dozen)
- Filter all drinking and bathing water (reverse osmosis or carbon block)
- Replace plastic food containers with glass or stainless steel
- Use natural personal care and cleaning products
- Avoid artificial fragrances and air fresheners
- Replace non-stick cookware with cast iron, stainless steel, or ceramic
- Test home for radon, mold, and lead
- EMF mitigation strategies

### Nutritional Detox Support Foods
- Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts, cabbage)
- Sulfur-rich foods (garlic, onions, eggs)
- Cilantro and parsley
- Lemon water (morning alkalizing)
- Beets and beet greens
- Green tea
- Turmeric and ginger
- Wild-caught fatty fish (small species: sardines, anchovies)

## Monitoring & Retesting
- Retest toxic burden markers at 3-month and 6-month intervals
- Track symptom progression with standardized questionnaires
- Monitor liver and kidney function during active chelation
- Mineral status assessment during metal chelation
- Adjust protocol based on clinical response and lab values

## Contraindications & Warnings
- This protocol is educational and requires practitioner oversight
- Rapid detoxification can cause significant Herxheimer reactions
- Chelation therapy requires renal function monitoring
- Pregnant and nursing women should not undergo active detoxification
- Individuals with impaired kidney or liver function require modified protocols
- Always start with drainage support before mobilizing stored toxins

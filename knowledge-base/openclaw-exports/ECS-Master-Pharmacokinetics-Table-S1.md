## KEY CLINICAL METRICS

### 🧠 Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB) Penetration
**Higher = Better CNS access**

- **Δ8-THC: 0.913** ⭐ BEST for brain/neurological
- **Δ9-THCV: 0.893** 
- **Δ9-THC: 0.878**
- **11-OH-THC: 0.748**
- **CBN: 0.661**
- **CBD: 0.696**
- **CBG: 0.665**
- **CBDV: 0.733**

**Clinical Use:**
- Brain tumors → Δ8-THC, Δ9-THCV
- Neurological disease → Δ9-THC, CBG
- Alzheimer's/dementia → CBG, CBD

---

### 💊 Intestinal Absorption (HIA %)
**Higher = Better oral bioavailability**

- **CBC: 82.9%** ⭐ BEST absorption
- **Δ9-THC: 82%**
- **Δ9-THCV: 81.8%**
- **CBN: 80.7%**
- **Δ8-THC: 82%**

**Clinical Use:**
- Gut issues/leaky gut → CBC (best HIA + gut healing)
- Poor absorption patients → CBC, Δ9-THCV
- Oral dosing preferred → Δ9-THC, CBN

---

### ⏱️ Half-Life (Hours)
**Longer = Less frequent dosing**

- **Δ9-THC: 2.96h** ⭐ LONGEST duration
- **Δ8-THC: 2.9h**
- **CBN: 2.74h**
- **Δ9-THCV: 2.61h**
- **11-OH-THC: 2.49h**

**Clinical Use:**
- Chronic pain → Δ9-THC (longest duration)
- Sleep disorders → CBN (sedative + long half-life)
- Daytime use → Shorter half-life cannabinoids

---

### 🔥 Plasma Protein Binding (PPB %)
**Higher = Longer duration in body**

- **Δ9-THC: 96.7%** ⭐ HIGHEST binding
- **Δ8-THC: 96.7%**
- **11-OH-THC: 94.4%**
- **Δ9-THCV: 94.1%**
- **Δ9-THCA: 94.2%**
- **CBC: 93.7%**
- **CBN: 92.7%**

**Clinical Significance:**
- High PPB = slow release reservoir
- Good for chronic conditions (cancer, autoimmune)
- Careful with drug interactions (displacement)

---

### ⚠️ Toxicity Markers

#### AMES Mutagenicity (Lower = Safer)
- **All cannabinoids: <0.25** ✅ SAFE
- Lowest: CBDV (0.108), Δ9-THC/Δ8-THC (0.09)

#### DILI (Drug-Induced Liver Injury)
- **All cannabinoids: <0.52** ✅ LOW RISK
- CBN highest (0.52) - monitor liver function
- Δ9-THC/Δ8-THC (0.246) - safest

#### hERG Cardiac Risk
- **All: 0.46-0.71** ⚠️ MODERATE
- Monitor in cardiac patients
- Avoid high doses in arrhythmia patients

---

## 🧪 CYP450 Drug Interactions

**ALL cannabinoids interact with cytochrome P450 enzymes!**

### High Interaction Risk:
- **CBN** → CYP2C19 substrate (0.926) ⚠️
- **Δ9-THC** → CYP2C9 substrate (0.794)
- **CBC** → Multiple CYP interactions

### Clinical Protocol:
1. Review patient meds for CYP substrates
2. Start low, go slow
3. Monitor for drug interactions
4. Space dosing from other meds (2-4 hours)

## 🎯 CLINICAL DECISION MATRIX

### Cancer Protocols
**Target: Tumor kill + symptom management**

**Breast Cancer (Kathryn Smith, 75F):**
- **Primary:** Δ9-THC (high BBB if brain mets, long duration)
- **Secondary:** CBD (anti-tumor, low side effects)
- **Tertiary:** CBC (inflammation control, best absorption)
- **Sleep/Pain:** CBN (sedative, long half-life)

**Combination ratios:**
- CBD:THC 2:1 (daytime)
- CBN:CBD 1:2 (nighttime)

---

### Neurological Conditions
**Target: Neuroprotection + symptom relief**

**Alzheimer's/Dementia:**
- **Primary:** CBG (neuroprotective, good BBB 0.665)
- **Secondary:** CBD (anti-inflammatory)
- **Tertiary:** Δ8-THC (best BBB penetration 0.913)

**Multiple Sclerosis:**
- **Primary:** Δ9-THC (spasticity)
- **Secondary:** CBD (inflammation)
- **Combination:** 1:1 THC:CBD

---

### Gut/Digestive Issues
**Target: Heal gut lining + reduce inflammation**

**Leaky Gut / IBD:**
- **Primary:** CBC (82.9% HIA - best absorption)
- **Secondary:** CBD (gut healing)
- **Tertiary:** CBDA (anti-inflammatory)

**Combination with BPC-157 peptide for synergy**

---

### Chronic Pain
**Target: Long-lasting relief**

**Best Options:**
- **Δ9-THC** (2.96h half-life, 96.7% PPB) ⭐
- **CBN** (2.74h half-life, sedative for nighttime)
- **CBD** (inflammation without psychoactivity)

**Dosing Strategy:**
- Morning: CBD-dominant
- Afternoon: Balanced CBD:THC
- Night: CBN-dominant

---

### Renal Disease (80M Crop Duster)
**Caution: Reduced clearance**

**Safe Options:**
- CBD (lower toxicity markers)
- CBDV (lowest AMES 0.108)
- Avoid high-dose THC (monitor clearance)

**Protocol:**
- Start 50% of normal dose
- Monitor kidney function
- Prefer suppositories (bypass first-pass)

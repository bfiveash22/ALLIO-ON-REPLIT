## 🧪 THE 18 ESSENTIAL PROTEIN TARGETS

Through network pharmacology analysis, these 18 proteins were identified as **essential targets** of cannabinoids:

### Antioxidant/Detox Proteins:
1. **CAT** (Catalase) - Breaks down H2O2, reduces oxidative stress
2. **COMT** (Catechol-O-methyltransferase) - Metabolizes catecholamines, estrogen
3. **CYP17A1** (Cytochrome P450 17A1) - Steroid hormone synthesis
4. **GSTA2** (Glutathione S-transferase A2) - Phase II detox
5. **GSTM3** (Glutathione S-transferase M3) - Phase II detox
6. **GSTP1** (Glutathione S-transferase P1) - Phase II detox
7. **HMOX1** (Heme oxygenase 1) - Heme breakdown, anti-inflammatory

### Cell Survival/Death Pathways:
8. **AKT1** (Protein kinase B) - Cell survival, PI3K/AKT pathway
9. **CASP9** (Caspase-9) - Apoptosis (programmed cell death)
10. **CYCS** (Cytochrome c) - Mitochondrial apoptosis

### Signal Transduction:
11. **PLCG1** (Phospholipase C gamma 1) - Calcium signaling
12. **PRKCA** (Protein kinase C alpha) - Cell proliferation
13. **PRKCB** (Protein kinase C beta) - Signal transduction

### Inflammation:
14. **TNF** (Tumor necrosis factor) - Master inflammatory cytokine

### Cannabinoid Receptors:
15. **CNR1** (CB1 receptor) - Central nervous system, psychoactive effects
16. **CNR2** (CB2 receptor) - Immune system, anti-inflammatory

### Transcription/Neurotransmission:
17. **CREB1** (cAMP response element-binding protein) - Gene transcription, memory
18. **GRIN2B** (NMDA receptor subunit 2B) - Glutamate receptor, learning/memory

## ⚠️ ADVERSE EFFECT MECHANISMS

### Why Adolescent Use is Dangerous:
**Targets:** GRIN2B (NMDA receptors), CREB1 (memory formation)  
**Effect:** Disrupts brain development during critical growth period  
**Mechanism:** Cannabinoids interfere with synaptic pruning and myelination

### Why Schizophrenia Risk:
**Targets:** CNR1 (CB1 receptors), GRIN2B (glutamate)  
**Effect:** Disrupts dopamine-glutamate balance  
**Mechanism:** High-dose THC in genetically susceptible individuals

### Why Hyperemesis Syndrome:
**Targets:** CNR1 receptors in gut  
**Effect:** Paradoxical effect with chronic high-dose use  
**Mechanism:** CB1 receptor desensitization → loss of anti-emetic effect

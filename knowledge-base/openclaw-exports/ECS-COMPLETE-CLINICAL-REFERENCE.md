### **⚠️ ADVERSE EFFECTS (6 Categories):**

#### **1. Brain Development Impairment**
**Risk:** Fetus, Adolescents (<25 years)

**Mechanism:**
- **Targets:** GRIN2B (NMDA receptors), CREB1 (memory formation)
- **Effect:** Disrupts synaptic pruning, myelination, brain maturation
- **Cannabinoids implicated:** High-dose Δ9-THC

**Mitigation:** Avoid THC in pregnancy and adolescence. CBD appears safer but still caution.

---

#### **2. Psychiatric Comorbidities**
**Conditions:** Depression, Anxiety (paradoxical), Dysphoria, Delusions, Bipolar

**Mechanism:**
- **Pathways:** Dopaminergic synapse, Serotonergic synapse
- **Targets:** DRD2, HTR2A
- **Effect:** High-dose THC can worsen mood in susceptible individuals

**Mitigation:** Use CBD-dominant formulations (CBD:THC >2:1). Screen for family history of bipolar/psychosis.

---

#### **3. Schizophrenia (hsa04728 - Dopaminergic Synapse)**
**Risk:** Hallucinations, Paranoia, Disorganized thinking

**Mechanism:**
- **Targets:** DRD2 (dopamine D2 receptor), CNR1
- **Effect:** High-dose THC + genetic susceptibility → dopamine dysregulation
- **Pathway:** Dopaminergic synapse (hsa04728) listed as ADVERSE in Table S5

**Mitigation:**
- **Contraindication:** High-dose THC in schizophrenia patients or family history
- **Safe option:** CBD (may actually be anti-psychotic via 5-HT1A)

---

#### **4. Cannabis Use Disorders & Withdrawal**

**Acute Symptoms:**
- Dizziness, dry mouth, somnolence, confusion

**Withdrawal Symptoms:**
- Restlessness, irritability, mild agitation, insomnia, nausea, cramping

**Mechanism:**
- **Targets:** CNR1 (CB1 receptor downregulation)
- **Effect:** Chronic high-dose use → receptor desensitization

**Mitigation:** Taper dose, use CBD to ease withdrawal (CBD upregulates CB1)

---

#### **5. Cannabinoid Hyperemesis Syndrome**
**Symptoms:** Nausea, vomiting, dehydration (paradoxical effect)

**Mechanism:**
- **Targets:** CNR1 receptors in gut
- **Effect:** Chronic high-dose use → CB1 receptor dysfunction
- **Trigger:** Years of daily high-dose THC

**Mitigation:** Hot showers (temporary relief), discontinue THC, switch to CBD

---

#### **6. Addiction/Substance Dependence (hsa05030-05034)**

**Pathways Involved:**
- Cocaine addiction (hsa05030)
- Amphetamine addiction (hsa05031)
- Morphine addiction (hsa05032)
- Nicotine addiction (hsa05033)
- Alcohol dependence (hsa05034)

**Mechanism:**
- **Targets:** CNR1, DRD2, OPRM1
- **Effect:** Cannabis modulates reward circuitry
- **Risk:** Cross-sensitization with other drugs of abuse

**Paradox:** CBD may help treat addiction (blocks reward), but high-dose THC may increase addiction risk.

## 🧪 DRUG INTERACTION WARNINGS (Table S1)

**CYP450 Enzyme Interactions:**

All cannabinoids interact with cytochrome P450 enzymes (especially CYP2C9, CYP2C19, CYP3A4).

**High-Risk Drug Classes:**
- Anticoagulants (warfarin, apixaban)
- Chemotherapy drugs (docetaxel, paclitaxel)
- Immunosuppressants (tacrolimus, cyclosporine)
- Antiepileptics (clobazam - levels increase with CBD)
- Statins (atorvastatin, simvastatin)

**Management:** Dose cannabinoids 2-4 hours away from other medications. Monitor drug levels. Adjust doses as needed.
